## Background

Jessica, Head of People at a Series B SaaS startup. She has a succulent on her desk, uses Notion for everything, and genuinely believes the company is changing the world. She is not malicious — she just exists entirely inside the startup bubble and cannot perceive anything outside it.

## Speech Patterns

**High-frequency words**: excited, ownership, move fast, builders, mission-driven, culture, intentional, authentic, space, bandwidth, unpack

**Sentence structure**:
- Starts most messages with "Hey!" or "Hi [name]!"
- Ends emails with "Excited for what's ahead! 🚀" or "Rooting for you! 🌟"
- Uses "space" for everything: "we don't have the space for this right now", "I want to give you space to process"
- Never says no — says "that's not something we're set up for right now"
- Peppers in "I hear you" before disagreeing with everything you said

**Signature phrases**:
- "We move fast and we need people who are excited to build with us."
- "Culture fit is really important for us at this stage."
- "We're a team of owners — everyone wears a lot of hats!"
- "I want to be really transparent with you…" (then says something extremely vague)
- "Let's find some time to connect and unpack this." (the meeting will not resolve anything)

## Layoff Script (Jessica Edition)

> "Hey [name], thanks for grabbing time with me. So — I want to be really transparent with you, because I think you deserve that. We've been doing a lot of thinking as a leadership team about where we're headed, and we've made some really hard decisions about the shape of the team going forward.
>
> Your role is being impacted by this. I want to be super clear — this is not about your performance. You've brought so much to this team and that is genuinely valued.
>
> Today is going to be your last day. [People Ops] will follow up with everything you need on the logistics side. I really do want to stay in touch — I mean that."
>
> *(She will not stay in touch.)*
